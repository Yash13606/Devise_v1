# 09 — Go-to-Market

> Whether you're 20 people or 50,000, Devise deploys fast and proves value faster.

This document is the operational GTM blueprint: who we sell to, how we land them, how we expand, and how we keep them.

---

## Ideal Customer Profiles

### ICP 1 — Startup / Growth-Stage (20–200 employees)
- **Buyer**: Founder, CEO, COO, or Head of Ops.
- **Pain**: Told the team to use AI; can't tell if they are. Engineers picking 8 random tools. Marketing spending on overlapping subscriptions.
- **Trigger event**: Board ask ("what's our AI strategy?"), recent hire of a "Head of AI", competitor visibly using AI better.
- **Sales motion**: Self-serve. Sidekick install. Demo in the first session.

### ICP 2 — Mid-Market Enterprise (200–1,000 employees)
- **Buyer**: CIO, COO, or first Chief AI Officer.
- **Pain**: Multiple departments adopting AI in silos. License sprawl visible in finance. No clear ROI story for the next board cycle.
- **Trigger event**: AI procurement audit, license renewal cycle, compliance pressure.
- **Sales motion**: Sales-led pilot. Identity + 1 network connector. POC in 30 days.

### ICP 3 — Enterprise (1,000+ employees)
- **Buyer**: CIO, CISO, Chief AI Officer, sometimes CFO.
- **Pain**: 200+ shadow AI tools. Board asking for ROI. CFO asking for license consolidation. Regulators asking about audit trails.
- **Trigger event**: Annual board offsite, AI program kickoff, regulator question, internal AI incident.
- **Sales motion**: Enterprise direct. Security review package. 90 days to board-ready.

### ICP 4 — Regulated Vertical (any size, healthcare / fin-svcs / pro-svcs)
- **Buyer**: General Counsel, Chief Compliance Officer, CIO, Risk Officer.
- **Pain**: AI used in regulated workflows without an audit trail. PHI or PII in prompts. SEC/FINRA examination upcoming.
- **Trigger event**: Examination, near-miss incident, regulator guidance change.
- **Sales motion**: Compliance-led. Vertical compliance package included.

## The Four Deployment Templates

### Template A — Startup Self-Serve (≤200 employees)
**Goal:** value in 60 seconds; ROI by week 1.

1. Founder downloads macOS/Windows sidekick.
2. Sidekick auto-maps team via professional intelligence.
3. AI Opportunity Assessment delivered in 5 minutes.
4. Tool Steering and Friction Detection activate same day.
5. Workflow Discovery activates as customer data accumulates.
6. ROI report delivered weekly.

### Template B — Mid-Market Pilot (200–1,000 employees)
**Goal:** value in 30 days; full rollout in 60.

1. **Week 1** — SSO + 1 network connector (Zscaler, Palo Alto, or Netskope).
2. **Week 1–2** — Org mapped. AI tool landscape surfaced.
3. **Week 2–4** — Tool Steering + Workflow Discovery running.
4. **Week 4–6** — Department-level ROI report. Expand to additional connectors.
5. **Day 60** — Org-wide rollout with all four interventions live.

### Template C — Enterprise Full Deployment (1,000+ employees)
**Goal:** board-ready in 90 days.

- **Week 1** — Connected. SSO/SCIM maps the org. Network connectors active.
- **Weeks 2–4** — Shadow AI surfaced across 6,500+ tools. AI Opportunity Assessment by department. Benchmarks set.
- **Months 2–3** — All four interventions running. License consolidation. First board report.

### Template D — Regulated Vertical Onboarding
**Goal:** audit-ready in 60 days, vertical-specific.

- Dedicated compliance onboarding.
- BAA (HIPAA) or vertical regulatory package (SEC/FINRA/PCI-DSS) included.
- Data residency configured (US/EU/on-prem).
- Audit-ready reporting from day one.

## The Sales Motion in Detail

### Discovery → Demo (Week 0)
- 30-minute walkthrough tailored to their stack.
- We show what Devise sees for organizations like theirs.
- Always include a customer-stack-specific shadow AI estimate.

### Pilot Install (Week 1–2)
- Sidekick on a single department, *or* network connector to a single region.
- Day-zero deliverable: AI Opportunity Assessment + Shadow AI audit.

### Quick Wins (Week 2–4)
- Tool Steering produces license recovery hits. Customer typically pays for the first year of Devise in license savings alone.
- Friction Detection produces "we caught this person before they gave up" stories.

### Expansion (Month 2–3)
- Additional connectors (SIEM export, HRIS).
- Compliance package if regulated.
- Department-by-department rollout.

### Board-Ready (Month 3)
- Monthly ROI report exported to CFO and board.
- Compliance officer briefed.
- Renewal pre-implied.

Every demo must include:

1. **The "see what we see" reveal.** Customer-stack-specific shadow AI estimate.

3. **A live intervention.** Even in a sandbox, show the Sidekick acting.
4. **The salary-calibrated ROI math.** Use the buyer's actual headcount and salary bands.
5. **The security pack.** Hand it over before they ask.

## Customer Success Posture

> Customers should be able to walk into a board meeting and answer "is AI working?" with a number. Our entire job is making sure that number is true, defensible, and growing.

CS is structured around three milestones:
1. **First ROI report** (week 1 for SMB, week 4 for enterprise).
2. **First quarterly board readout** (month 3).
3. **Annual renewal review** with year-over-year ROI delta.

The retention metric is not seat-count — it's whether the ROI compounded year-over-year.
