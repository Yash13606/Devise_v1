# Data Governance

The framework of policies, processes, and standards that ensure data is managed as a valuable asset, maintaining its quality, security, privacy, and compliance.

Also known asInformation GovernanceData Management

Full Definition

## What is Data Governance?

Data governance is the overall management of data availability, usability, integrity, and security in an organization. It establishes policies, procedures, and standards for handling data throughout its lifecycle.

### Core Components

**Data Quality**

- Accuracy and completeness
- Consistency and validity
- Timeliness and relevance

**Data Security**

- Access controls
- Encryption
- Audit trails

**Data Privacy**

- Consent management
- Data subject rights
- Privacy compliance

**Data Lifecycle**

- Creation and collection
- Storage and processing
- Retention and disposal

### Key Roles

**Data Steward**

- Day-to-day data management
- Quality monitoring
- Policy enforcement

**Data Owner**

- Business accountability
- Access decisions
- Policy approval

**Data Custodian**

- Technical management
- Security implementation
- System administration

**Data Governance Council**

- Strategy and oversight
- Policy development
- Issue resolution

### Implementation Framework

1. **Strategy**


   - Goals and objectives
   - Scope definition

2. **Organization**


   - Roles and responsibilities
   - Governance structure

3. **Policies**


   - Standards and guidelines
   - Procedures

4. **Technology**


   - Data catalogs
   - Quality tools
   - Metadata management

5. **Metrics**


   - KPIs
   - Monitoring
   - Reporting
