# Data Privacy

The right of individuals to control how their personal information is collected, used, shared, and stored, along with the organizational practices that protect this right.

Also known asInformation PrivacyPersonal Data Protection

Full Definition

## What is Data Privacy?

Data privacy refers to the proper handling of personal data concerning consent, notice, and regulatory obligations. It encompasses the rights of individuals to control their personal information and the responsibilities of organizations to protect that information.

### Key Principles

**Notice**
Inform individuals about data collection practices.

**Choice**
Allow individuals to opt in/out of data collection.

**Access**
Enable individuals to view their collected data.

**Security**
Protect personal data from unauthorized access.

**Integrity**
Maintain accuracy of personal data.

**Enforcement**
Implement mechanisms to ensure compliance.

### Privacy by Design

1. Proactive not reactive
2. Privacy as the default
3. Privacy embedded in design
4. Full functionality
5. End-to-end security
6. Visibility and transparency
7. Respect for user privacy

### Key Regulations

- GDPR (European Union)
- CCPA/CPRA (California)
- LGPD (Brazil)
- POPIA (South Africa)
- PDPA (Singapore/Thailand)
